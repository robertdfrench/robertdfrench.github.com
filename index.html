About Me

I am a software engineer, mathematician, researcher, and aspiring math educator. I believe that improving and spreading math literacy is an important goal, and that mathematics is vital to understanding the complex social, economic, and environmental issues of the 21st century. I also believe that quality software can be a tremendous tool for helping educators achieve this goal.

Open Source

I believe in the power of open source software. The free sharing of technology can help people solve problems both for themselves and one another. I expect freely available, quality software will be behind many advances in scientific computing and educational technology over the next several years.

Experience

I have been developing web applications professionally for almost 10 years. I am a fan of REST and other paradigms for interconnecting web services, as I believe that they help foster interconnectivity, and importantly they allow for web applications to support many client front ends, such as specialized accessible front-ends for differently abled persons. The old style of all-in-one web applications that generate HTML as request responses neither scale well nor lend themselves to addressing such usability issues.

Scientific Research

My scientific research focuses on Genetic Algorithms, Finite Element Analysis, and Numerov-style ODE solvers. My paper with Dr. Samuel Jator and Scott Swindell introducing a "Trigonometrically fitted block Numerov type method" was recently published in the journal Numerical Algorithms and is available online here. In the future I hope to begin studying teaching techniques in elementary math education, and the impacts of poverty on mathematical achievement and literacy.
