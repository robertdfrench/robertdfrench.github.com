Mathematica Book

Together with my advisor, Dr. Samuel Jator, I am writing An Introduction to Mathematica Programming for Undergraduate Research. The goal of the book is to help undergraduate science students develop the functional and symbolic programming skills needed to excel in computational research.

Scientific Research

My scientific research focuses on Genetic Algorithms, Finite Element Analysis, Numerov-style ODE Solvers, and Backward Differentation Formulae. My paper with Dr. Samuel Jator and Scott Swindell introducing a Trigonometrically fitted block Numerov type method was recently published in the journal, Numerical Algorithms.

In 2011, my research with Casey McKnight (Stanford) and Amber O'Connell (OSU) on a massively parallel implementation of the Finite Element Method was presented in the Numerical Analysis section of the AMS-MAA Joint Mathematics Meeting in New Orleans, LA. The Bamboo software improves the performance of the classical FEM by distributing construction of the Stiffness Matrix across a cluster of Mathematica nodes.

Math Education Projects

I believe that improving and spreading math literacy is an important goal, and that mathematics is vital to understanding the complex social, economic, and environmental issues of the 21st century. I also believe that quality software can be a tremendous tool for helping educators achieve this goal. To that end, I am collaborating with Justin Lauria (Zynga), J. Caleb Wherry (VA Tech), and Liza C. Kurtz (APSU) to develop an adaptive, collaborative web-based math workbook targeted at 5th-9th grade students. The Math Academy software works by traversing a skill dependency graph to determine where students most need help.

In the future I hope to begin researching new teaching techniques in elementary math education, algorithms for adaptive web-based math education tools, and the impacts of poverty on mathematical achievement and literacy.
